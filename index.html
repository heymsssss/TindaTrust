      The flow, end to end

      Three steps, under two minutes

      This is the exact order a judge would see in a live demo.

      01 · customer

      Bili sa utang

      Customer buys on credit at the counter, like always. Nothing changes for them.

      02 · on-chain

      Naka-record na

      Owner taps "record utang." A Soroban contract call updates that customer's balance, publicly and permanently.

      03 · settlement

      Bayad, malinaw

      Customer repays in cash or USDC. Balance drops to zero on-chain — no argument, no lost notebook page.

      Why Stellar, specifically

      Built for ₱20 transactions, not ₱20,000 ones

      Fees low enough, and settlement fast enough, that tracking small everyday credit actually makes sense.

      Soroban contracts
      Runs the utang ledger logic itself — recording debt, enforcing repayment limits, storing balances per customer.
      Custom tokens
      A store-issued credit asset customers can eventually hold, trade, or redeem across neighboring sari-sari stores.
      Trustlines
      Customers explicitly opt in to a store's credit asset — no one is handed a token they didn't agree to.
      USDC settlement
      Real repayment happens in a stable asset, so the store owner isn't exposed to volatility for day-to-day cash flow.
